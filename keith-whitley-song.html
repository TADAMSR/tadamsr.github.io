I Need a Keith Whitley Song

Written by Thomas R. Adams

Verse 1

I need a sad song,
Or maybe a Marty Robbins ballad all night long.
Play me some Junior Sisk
To help these brand-new shoes of mine to fit.
I ain’t gonna ask twice,
No need for advice
Just play another Bob Dylan hit.

Chorus

Well, these nights keep rollin’ on,
I’m hoppin’ trains like a Jerry Jeff song,
Singin’ “Hello Walls” like Willie and Faron Young.
And when the quiet hits too strong,
My wishful thinkin’ proves me wrong,
And I hear angels hummin’ Unchained Melody.

And if there’s one thing I still chase,
It’s where the stars don’t fall at all…
Just a voice that cuts straight through the haze…
Lord, I need a Keith Whitley song.

Verse 2

Yeah, I’m no stranger to the rain,
Darlin’, don’t close your eyes again.
I still see you smilin’ somewhere down in Miami now.
And when you say nothin’ at all,
It makes this empty world feel small 
’Cause that was love from only ten feet away somehow.

Chorus

Well, these nights keep rollin’ on,
I’m hoppin’ trains like a Jerry Jeff song,
Singin’ Hello Walls like Willie and Faron Young.
And when the quiet hits too strong,
My wishful thinkin’ proves me wrong,
And I hear angels hummin’ Unchained Melody.

And if there’s one thing I still chase,
It’s where the stars don’t fall at all…
Just a voice that cuts straight through the haze…
Lord, I need a Keith Whitley song.

Bridge

Maybe someday the hurt will mend,
But tonight I need an old friend…
Yeah, pour me somethin’ strong —
And play that Keith Whitley song.

Final Chorus / Outro

Lord, these nights keep rollin’ on,
I’m hoppin’ trains like a Jerry Jeff song
Singin’ Hello Walls like Willie and Faron Young.
And when the quiet hits too strong,
My wishful thinkin’ proves me wrong,
And I hear angels hummin’ Unchained Melody.

And if there’s one thing I still chase,
It’s where the stars don’t fall at all…
Just a voice that cuts straight through the haze…
Lord, I need a Keith Whitley song.

Just a Keith Whitley song.
