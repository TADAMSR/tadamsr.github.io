Memory Worth Remembering

Written by Thomas R. Adams

Chorus

I wanna be a postcard you send home to mama,
From a little town you’ve never been before.
I wanna be a picture tucked in a scrapbook,
One with a story you’ve never told before.
And I don’t mind bein’ dusted off if I’m stowed away,
As long as I get opened up sometime.

Verse 1

I ain’t askin’ to be some treasure on your mantel,
Or somethin’ sacred that you guard with your whole heart.
Just a small reminder folded in your pocket,
Proof that even little moments can leave a mark.

Pre-Chorus

I don’t need to be a prayer you whisper when you hit your knees at night,
Or some dream you chase when the nightmares get too rough.
I don’t need to be an heirloom passed down through the years—
Just somethin’ worth recallin’ when the quiet draws you near.

Maybe I’m that old vinyl you keep for the memories,
The kind that crackles when the melody comes through.
Or a worn-down pocketknife your daddy handed over,
Ain’t much to look at, but it still holds up—same as when he handed it to you.
